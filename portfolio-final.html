About

🎓

I am an undergraduate student in Electrical and Electronic Engineering, working at the intersection of Biomedical Engineering, Quantum Photonics, and Computational Neuroscience. My research is interdisciplinary, investigating both fundamental principles and applied implementations of physics involving quantum optics, nanophotonics, and neural signal processing.

Broadly, my research is concerned with understanding and developing scalable, chip-integrated photonic quantum technologies for use in medical diagnostics and quantum information processing. My work is primarily experimental and computational, where day-to-day I am involved with simulation development, biomedical signal analysis using machine learning, and photonic device design.

Prior to focusing on quantum photonics, I completed several research projects in biomedical signal processing, including EEG-based memory decoding and emotion recognition, which were published in peer-reviewed venues. My undergraduate research experience has focused on bridging the gap between theoretical physics and practical healthcare applications.

Research Interests

My research spans several interconnected areas in quantum physics and biomedical engineering, with applications in quantum information science, medical diagnostics, and neural decoding.

Quantum Photonics

Investigation of quantum phenomena in photonic systems, exploring the quantum nature of light in integrated photonic circuits. Rather than attempting to eliminate losses, I am developing techniques to harness quantum properties for scalable quantum computing and long-distance quantum communication applications.

Nanophotonics

Exploring how light behaves and propagates within materials at the nanoscale. My research involves designing, simulating, fabricating and characterizing nanophotonic devices including metasurfaces, optical tweezers, and integrated waveguide systems for sensing and manipulation applications.

Computational Neuroscience

Decoding neural signals using advanced signal processing and machine learning techniques. My work focuses on understanding cognitive states through EEG analysis, developing explainable AI frameworks for interpreting brain activity, and building brain-computer interface systems for medical applications.

Biomedical Optics

Application of optical techniques for medical diagnostics and therapeutic interventions at the cellular level. This includes developing optical imaging systems, studying light-tissue interactions, and creating photonic biosensors for disease detection and monitoring.
