About

        
     
                🎓      
            
       

          I am an undergraduate student in Electrical and Electronic Engineering at CUET, working at the intersection of            Biomedical Engineering, Quantum Photonics,            and Computational Neuroscience. My research is interdisciplinary, investigating            both fundamental principles and applied implementations of physics involving quantum optics, nanophotonics,            and neural signal processing.        

       

          Broadly, my research is concerned with understanding and developing scalable, chip-integrated photonic quantum            technologies for use in medical diagnostics and quantum information processing. My work is primarily experimental            and computational, where day-to-day I am involved with simulation development (COMSOL, Lumerical FDTD), biomedical            signal analysis using machine learning, and photonic device design.        

       

          Prior to focusing on quantum photonics and metasurface design, I completed several research projects in biomedical            signal processing, including large-scale EEG-based memory decoding and emotion recognition using explainable AI,            which were published in peer-reviewed venues. My undergraduate research experience has focused on bridging the gap            between theoretical physics and practical healthcare applications.        

     
   
 
   
   

Research Interests

   

      My research spans several interconnected areas in quantum physics and biomedical engineering,        with applications in quantum information science, medical diagnostics, and neural decoding.    

        
           
       
                 
       
         

Quantum Photonics

         

            Investigation of quantum phenomena in photonic systems, exploring the quantum nature of light              in integrated photonic circuits. Developing techniques to harness quantum properties for scalable              quantum computing and long-distance quantum communication applications using chip-scale platforms.          

       
     
           
       
                 
       
         

Nanophotonics & Metasurfaces

         

            Exploring how light behaves and propagates within materials at the nanoscale. Research involves              designing, simulating, and characterizing nanophotonic devices including reconfigurable metasurfaces,              optical tweezers, and integrated waveguide systems utilizing phase-change materials for dynamic control.          

       
     
           
       
                 
       
         

Computational Neuroscience

         

            Decoding neural signals using advanced signal processing and machine learning techniques.              Focus on understanding cognitive states through EEG analysis, developing explainable AI frameworks              for interpreting brain activity, and building brain-computer interface systems for medical applications.          

       
     
           
       
                 
       
         

Biomedical Optics

         

            Application of optical techniques for medical diagnostics and therapeutic interventions at              the cellular level. Includes developing optical imaging systems, studying light-tissue              interactions, and creating photonic biosensors for disease detection and real-time monitoring.

Past Research Projects

        
           
       
         

            Decoding Memory with Explainable AI: A Large-Scale EEG-Based Machine Learning Study of Encoding vs. Retrieval          

         
            📅 Feb. 2024 – June 2025             Published          
       
                
             
  • Implementation of machine learning techniques on EEG data from the Penn Electrophysiology of Encoding and Retrieval Study (PEERS), involving 100 participants across more than 400 sessions.
    •          
  • Utilization of Discrete Wavelet Transform (DWT) to analyze EEG signals from six critical brain regions during memory encoding and retrieval.
    •          
  • Application of Explainable AI (XAI) methods, specifically SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP), to interpret neurophysiological markers of memory processes.
    •        
     
           
       
         

            Spectrogram-Driven Emotion Detection from Electroencephalogram          

         
            📅 July 2024 – Dec. 2024             Published          
       
                
             
  • Employed Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) to convert EEG signals into time-frequency spectrograms for CNN-based emotion recognition.
    •          
  • Achieved 99.80% classification accuracy for emotional states (positive, negative, neutral) using processed spectrogram representations.
    •          
  • Demonstrated reduced performance (66%) when using raw spectrograms, highlighting the importance of preprocessing.
    •        
                
         

Supervisor: Md. Fazlul Karim Khondakar, Assistant Professor, Department of Biomedical Engineering, CUET.
